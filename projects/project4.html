Dynamic Vibration Analysis & Dampening System

Industry: Industrial Machinery Duration: 4 months Role: Vibration Analysis Engineer

Project Overview

A critical piece of industrial processing equipment was experiencing severe vibration issues leading to premature bearing failures, structural fatigue, and unplanned downtime. This project involved comprehensive modal analysis, root cause identification, and design of a custom vibration dampening solution.

Technical Challenge

The equipment was operating at speeds that excited multiple resonance frequencies:

  • Excessive vibration amplitudes (>50mm/s RMS) at operating speeds
  • Multiple resonance frequencies within the operating range (1200-1800 RPM)
  • Bearing failures occurring every 3-4 months instead of expected 2-year life
  • Structural cracks developing in mounting brackets and support frames
  • Equipment shutdowns causing significant production losses
  • Space and weight constraints limiting dampening solutions

Approach & Methodology

1. Diagnostic Phase

  • Installed tri-axial accelerometers at critical measurement points
  • Conducted experimental modal analysis (EMA) using impact hammer testing
  • Performed operating deflection shape (ODS) analysis during normal operation
  • Collected time-domain and frequency-domain vibration data using LabVIEW
  • Identified natural frequencies, mode shapes, and damping ratios

2. Computational Modal Analysis

  • Created detailed finite element model in ANSYS including all structural components
  • Performed modal analysis to predict natural frequencies and mode shapes
  • Validated FE model by correlating with experimental results (MAC > 0.95)
  • Conducted harmonic response analysis to predict forced vibration behavior
  • Evaluated multiple design modifications using parametric studies

3. Solution Design

  • Designed tuned mass damper system targeting primary resonance frequency
  • Specified viscoelastic isolators for base mounting with optimized stiffness/damping
  • Reinforced structural members to shift natural frequencies away from operating range
  • Created detailed engineering drawings and installation procedures

4. Implementation & Validation

  • Fabricated and installed dampening components during scheduled maintenance
  • Conducted post-modification vibration testing and analysis
  • Monitored long-term vibration levels and bearing condition
  • Documented baseline improvement for future reference

Technical Details

Primary Resonance Identified: The first bending mode of the main shaft assembly occurred at 1,450 RPM, directly within the operating speed range. This mode exhibited a damping ratio of only 0.8%, making it highly susceptible to resonance.

Dampening Solution: A tuned mass damper was designed with a natural frequency of 1,440 RPM and optimized damping ratio of 12%. This absorber effectively "pulled" vibration energy from the main structure, reducing response amplitudes by 85%.

Isolation System: Replacing rigid mounts with properly sized viscoelastic isolators provided additional damping and prevented vibration transmission to the foundation, addressing secondary issues with floor vibration.

Key Learnings

This project demonstrated the critical importance of experimental validation in vibration analysis. While FEA provided valuable insights, the experimental modal analysis revealed complexities in the actual system behavior that weren't captured in the initial models, including significant contribution from piping and auxiliary equipment.

The combination of multiple mitigation strategies proved more effective than any single approach. The tuned mass damper addressed the primary resonance, while structural reinforcement and isolation tackled secondary issues. This holistic approach is now our standard methodology for vibration problems.
